Control Flow and Loops

Control flow is a flow in which a computer executes lines of code in a script. This flow starts from the first line on the top and runs through to the last line on the bottom of a script. However there could be indicators that control the flow in other directions. (e.g loop, conditional statement.).

The control flow can be explained by looking at the reading sentences. While control flow starts from the first line to the last top to bottom, we are reading sentences form left to right for a sentence and top to bottom for paragraphs. However, if I told you to find a typo in previous sentences and do not read further until a typo is found, you will need to go back to the previous sentence and read through them again until you find one.

Reading in order is control flow and reading through from start repeatedly until a condition is met (in this case finding a typo) is a loop.
